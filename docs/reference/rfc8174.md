RFC 8174 updates [RFC 2119](rfc2119.md) and clarifies how BCP 14 requirement
keywords are interpreted in specifications.

The rule it adds: requirement keywords such as `MUST`, `SHOULD`, and `MAY` have
their defined BCP 14 meanings only when written in all capitals, unless a
specification explicitly defines a different convention. Lowercase words such
as "must", "should", and "may" keep their ordinary English meaning.

RFC 8174 also adds `NOT RECOMMENDED` to the standard keyword list and gives the
modern citation boilerplate for specifications that use BCP 14 keywords.
