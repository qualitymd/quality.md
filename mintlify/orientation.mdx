This is the page that ties QUALITY.md together. Orientation isn't a one-time
setup step — it's the place you keep returning to as your project and your
understanding of its quality change.

QUALITY.md is built around a set of nested loops that move quality judgment up
the loop stack instead of relying on ad hoc prompts and reactive reviews. Make
that judgment explicit once, and every loop below it gets faster and more
consistent.

## The Quality Loop Stack

{/* TODO: Expand each loop and its cadence. Keep the named-loop capitalization
    (Inner Loop, Middle Loop, Meta Loop, Outer Loop). */}

The stack is one workflow — evaluate, review, act, improve — running at four
cadences at once. Each loop hands its output up or down to the next.

- **The Middle / Meta Loop** (hourly / daily / weekly) — evaluate, then review
  the report. This is where quality judgment is made explicit and kept current.
- **The Inner Loop** (continuous) — act on handoff-ready recommendations as you
  work, with the model as shared context.
- **The Outer Loop** (daily / weekly) — improve by refining the quality model
  and raising the bar, compounding learning and maximizing leverage.

> The three-loop framing and the term *middle loop* draw on Annie Vella,
> *[The Middle Loop](https://annievella.com/posts/the-middle-loop/)*. The
> loop-engineering framing also draws on Latent Space,
> *[Loopcraft: The Art of Stacking Loops](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-loopcraft-the-art-of-stacking)*.

## Run your first loop

{/* TODO: Walk through running the loop on a real project end to end,
    harness-agnostic. Expand each step. */}

You run the loop through the `/quality` skill, harness-agnostic. One pass
through all four steps is your first loop.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Evaluate">
    Run `/quality evaluate` to produce an evaluation report — quality ratings,
    assessment findings, and recommendations across your project.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review">
    Read ratings, findings, and recommendations by area or factor. Decide what
    matters now.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Act">
    Implement or delegate handoff-ready recommendations to people, agents, or
    other agent loops.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Improve">
    Run `/quality improve` to refine `QUALITY.md` so the next loop starts from a
    sharper model.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Then run it again. The model you sharpen in *Improve* is what your next loop
reads from — that compounding is the point.
